Hello, and welcome to my site.

I'm Yusif, a Ph.D. student in Robotics at the University of Colorado Boulder, where I focus on safe and reliable autonomy as part of the ARIA Systems Group. My research centers on developing scalable algorithms for the task and motion planning of dynamic agents operating in complex environments, with an emphasis on multi-objective planning and multi-agent coordination.

I completed my M.S. in Aerospace Engineering at CU Boulder and hold dual bachelor's degrees in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering from West Virginia University, where I graduated Summa Cum Laude. My research and projects span a range of areas including motion-planning, formal methods, reinforcement learning, state-estimation, and embedded systems.

Previously, I worked at NASA's Independent Verification and Validation Facility, where I contributed to system modeling and validation efforts for the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope. I also founded Sneaker Space, a technology-driven e-commerce business, developing custom automation tools along the way. If you're interested in my work, please feel free to reach out.

— Yusif

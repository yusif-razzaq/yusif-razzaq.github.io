Hello, I'm Yusif.

I'm a Ph.D. student in Robotics at the University of Colorado Boulder, where I focus on motion planning and decision-making for autonomous systems. My work centers on developing algorithms that enable reliable and efficient behavior in complex environments, with an emphasis on multi-objective planning and multi-agent coordination. I completed my M.S. in Aerospace Engineering at CU Boulder and hold dual bachelor's degrees in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering from West Virginia University, where I graduated Summa Cum Laude. My research and projects span a range of areas including sampling-based motion-planning, multi-agent path-finding, formal methods, reinforcement learning, and embedded robotic systems. Previously, I worked at NASA's Independent Verification and Validation Facility, where I contributed to system modeling and validation efforts for the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope. I've also built and scaled a technology-driven e-commerce business, developing automation tools and software systems along the way. If you're interested in my work, please feel free to reach out!
